Food waste

One of FN’s Sustainable Development Goals, number 12.3 which concerns food waste, want to halve the food waste measured in kilos per inhabitant, applying to the groceries-industries and to the consumers.


Picture taken from: http://www.ntfe.no/i/2019/2/tfe-2019-02b-1395


FN has estimated that roughly one third of all the food we consume never gets eaten. In 2017, Østfoldsforskning measured in a reasearch they did on food waste, that every inhabitant in Norway that year threw away 73 kilos of edible food. This amount of food waste equals 22 billion Norwegian kroners when measured in kroners, and an climate-emission of 1.3 million CO2-equivalents per year. 42 kiloes of the total amount of 73 kiloes, were thrown away by Norwegian householdings. In other words, the householdings struggle the most on keeping food waste to a minimum.


Picture taken from: http://www.ntfe.no/i/2019/2/tfe-2019-02b-1395


The outcome of the research Østfoldforskning did in 2017, showed that 20% of the total edible food waste contained bread and pasteries. Casserole-dishes did have an outcome at 19%, while fruit and vegetables scored 18%.


CoMeals wishes to contribiute to achieve the goal for halve food waste in 2030. We do think that making lunch and other dishes to eachother will help reduce the amount of food waste, through becomming more observant on how much food you need for a small group, and that you don’t make more than necessary. We do also believe that making food is a social activity, and that CoMeals can facilitate so people not only get to experience new food, but also making contact with other fellowmen.
